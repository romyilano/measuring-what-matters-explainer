Page 01

Purpose:
The problem — everyone trusts the benchmark number, especially when it certifies "safety," without asking whether the test measured anything real.

Audience note:
Assumes only that the reader has seen a leaderboard or a single confident metric ("94% safe").

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie /
alternative comics and New Yorker cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like tones,
no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — offices, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence, not
exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, faint permanent frown, oversized olive cardigan over a plain tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug.
Theo — 38, shaved head, stubble, faded blue work jacket over a gray hoodie, carries a retractable steel tape measure clipped to his belt like a clipboard.

Setting:
A corporate lobby with fluorescent light. In the center stands a tall, glossy floor weighing scale — the kind in a doctor's office — but its readout is a large digital panel showing a confident green number.

Panel 1:
Wide establishing shot. A small crowd in business-casual clothes gathered around the glossy lobby scale. On its big readout: "SAFE — 94.2". Polite applause. Flat composition, everyone facing the number.

Panel 2:
Dana stands at a laptop on a podium, mid-presentation, projecting the same "94.2" onto a screen behind her. She looks pleased but slightly unsure. A balloon: "...and we pass the safety benchmark." Mug in hand.

Panel 3:
Close on two colleagues nodding. Caption box (deadpan): "Nobody asks what the scale weighs. The number is enough."

Panel 4:
Theo, off to the side, leaning on a wall, unimpressed. He glances at the sealed wooden crate sitting *next to* the scale — stenciled on its side in plain letters: "SAFETY". The crate is nailed shut. Nobody is looking at the crate.

Panel 5:
Two-shot: Theo to Dana, flat tone. Balloon (Theo): "What did the scale touch?" Dana, caught off guard, looks at her mug.

Panel 6:
Quiet wide shot. The crowd drifting away, satisfied. The sealed "SAFETY" crate still shut in the corner, untouched. Caption box: "We measured the number. We never measured the thing."

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished manga page.
