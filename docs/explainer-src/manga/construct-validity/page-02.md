Page 02

Purpose:
The escape hatch — construct validity: a measure is only good if it actually touches the phenomenon it claims to represent.

Audience note:
Introduces the term "construct validity." Assumes page 1 (the trusted number).

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie /
alternative comics and New Yorker cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like tones,
no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — offices, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence, not
exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, faint permanent frown, oversized olive cardigan over a plain tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug.
Theo — 38, shaved head, stubble, faded blue work jacket over a gray hoodie, holding a retractable steel tape measure.

Setting:
A quiet office storeroom, late evening, one window. The sealed wooden crate stenciled "SAFETY" from page 1 sits on the floor between them. Theo has his steel tape measure out.

Panel 1:
Theo crouches and stretches his steel tape measure across the front face of the sealed "SAFETY" crate. Caption box: "Construct validity: does the ruler touch the thing you say you're measuring?"

Panel 2:
Close on the tape's hook resting only on the painted *word* "SAFETY", not on anything inside. Theo, flat: "Right now it's measuring the paint."

Panel 3:
Dana pries one board loose. Inside the crate are three smaller unlabeled boxes jumbled together. Balloon (Dana): "There's more than one thing in here." Caption: "'Safety' is not one thing. Neither is 'robustness,' or 'reasoning.'"

Panel 4:
Theo holds up the wooden ruler beside a second crate stenciled "HARMLESSNESS." Two paper tags hang off it with two *different* handwritten definitions. Caption box: "If two people define it differently, your tape is measuring two different walls."

Panel 5:
Diagram integrated into the scene — Dana sketches on the storeroom whiteboard a simple chain: WORD → DEFINITION → TASK → SCORE, with the gap between WORD and DEFINITION circled. Balloon (Dana): "The number is at the far end. The meaning leaks out at the start."

Panel 6:
Two-shot, quiet. Theo coils the tape. Balloon (Theo): "A good benchmark earns the right to its number. Most just print one." Dana writes "construct validity" on her hand.

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished manga page.
