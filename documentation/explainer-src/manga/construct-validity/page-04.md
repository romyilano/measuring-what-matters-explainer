Page 04

Purpose:
The hidden part — the numbers people skip: exact-match scoring (81%), almost no uncertainty reported (16%), contested definitions, and data contamination.

Audience note:
The "back room" page. Lands the uncomfortable statistics from the review using physical props.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie /
alternative comics and New Yorker cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like tones,
no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — offices, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence, not
exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, faint permanent frown, oversized olive cardigan over a plain tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug.
Theo — 38, shaved head, stubble, faded blue work jacket over a gray hoodie.

Setting:
A cramped windowless back room behind the warehouse — the grading room. A bulky bubble-sheet (scantron) grading machine, a wall gauge with a single needle, a metal exam desk.

Panel 1:
Establishing shot of the grading machine chewing through stacks of bubble sheets. A handwritten sticky note on it: "81% — EXACT MATCH ONLY." Caption box: "Most tests check one thing: did the bubble line up. Not whether the answer made sense."

Panel 2:
Close on a bubble sheet: a thoughtful, correct hand-written answer in the margin, marked WRONG because it didn't match the key's exact wording. Dana frowns over it, mug halfway up. Balloon (Dana): "It's right. It's just not identical."

Panel 3:
Theo points at a wall gauge — a single needle, no error bars, no shaded band of uncertainty. A small plate under it: "ONLY 16% REPORT UNCERTAINTY." Balloon (Theo): "One needle, no wobble. They never say how sure they are."

Panel 4:
Two crates side by side, both stenciled "HARMLESSNESS", each with a different definition tag (echo of page 2). A tally chalk mark on the wall: "~48% OF DEFINITIONS CONTESTED." Caption box: "Same word. Two meanings. Averaged into one score."

Panel 5:
On the exam desk, an answer key lies face-up next to the blank test — the answer key left in the room. Caption box (deadpan): "And sometimes the model already saw the answers. Contamination. The exam, taken open-book, reported as closed."

Panel 6:
Pull back: Dana and Theo standing in the small room, the machine still running behind them. Balloon (Dana, flat): "So the impressive number is..." Theo, not unkind: "Real. Just not about what you think." Caption box: "Half the tests never justified that their number meant anything at all."

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished manga page.
