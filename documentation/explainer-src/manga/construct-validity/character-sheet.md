# Character sheet — construct-validity

Restate each character's visual traits **inline** in every page prompt they appear in.
Image models have no memory between pages; consistency comes from repetition.

## Dana — the skeptical learner (POV)
- **Role:** an ML researcher who shipped the leaderboard slide and is starting to doubt it.
- **Age:** 34.
- **Appearance:** black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, faint permanent frown.
- **Clothing:** oversized olive cardigan over a plain tee.
- **Distinguishing prop:** a chipped ceramic mug she never puts down.
- **Personality:** tired, dry, honest; asks the question everyone else skips.
- **Purpose:** stands in for the reader — wants the number to mean something.

## Theo — the deadpan measurement scientist (mentor / foil)
- **Role:** a metrologist; quietly smug; cares about whether instruments touch reality.
- **Age:** 38.
- **Appearance:** shaved head, stubble.
- **Clothing:** faded blue work jacket over a gray hoodie.
- **Distinguishing prop:** a retractable steel tape measure he carries like a clipboard.
- **Personality:** flat affect, precise, never raises his voice; lands the dry truths.
- **Purpose:** keeps asking "what are you actually measuring?"

## The system, drawn as mundane props (NEVER mascots)
- **A benchmark** → a glossy lobby weighing scale that prints a confident number.
- **The phenomenon ("safety", "robustness")** → a sealed wooden crate with a vague word stenciled on the side; nobody has opened it.
- **Construct validity** → a steel tape measure / tailor's tape actually laid against the thing.
- **The 445 benchmarks** → identical cardboard records boxes filling a warehouse.
- **Exact-match scoring** → a bubble-sheet (scantron) grading machine that only checks the filled circle.
- **Uncertainty / statistics** → a gauge needle; valid work has error bars, most work has none.
- **Data contamination** → the answer key left face-up in the exam room.
- **The eight recommendations** → eight plain index cards pinned to a corkboard; a tailor measuring properly.
