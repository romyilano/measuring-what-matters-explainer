Purpose:
The safety — the eight recommendations that give a benchmark construct validity, shown as a plain checklist done properly.

Audience note:
The constructive turn. Eight concrete fixes from the paper, made physical.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie /
alternative comics and New Yorker cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like tones,
no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — offices, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence, not
exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, faint permanent frown, oversized olive cardigan over a plain tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug.
Theo — 38, shaved head, stubble, faded blue work jacket over a gray hoodie, holding the steel tape measure again.

Setting:
Back in the storeroom, now tidied. A large corkboard on the wall. Theo pins eight plain index cards in two columns of four. Good light.

Panel 1:
Wide shot of the corkboard with all eight index cards pinned, numbered 1–8, each with a short hand-lettered title. Caption box: "Eight ways to earn the number." (Cards, in order, legible: 1 DEFINE THE PHENOMENON, 2 MEASURE ONLY THAT, 3 SAMPLE THE REAL TASK, 4 OWN YOUR REUSED DATA, 5 CHECK FOR CONTAMINATION, 6 REPORT UNCERTAINTY, 7 DO ERROR ANALYSIS, 8 JUSTIFY IT MATTERS.)

Panel 2:
Card 1 + 2 in focus. Theo lays the steel tape against the now-opened crate, this time touching one clearly labeled box inside. Balloon (Theo): "Say exactly what you mean. Then measure that and nothing next to it." Caption: "1 — define it. 2 — don't let other skills sneak onto the scale."

Panel 3:
Card 3 + 4. Dana scoops a representative handful of items from a big bin labeled "THE REAL TASK" rather than grabbing the nearest box. A reused box is re-labeled with an honest tag: "borrowed — here's what it's good for." Caption: "3 — sample the real world, not what's handy. 4 — if you reuse a dataset, say what it can't do."

Panel 4:
Card 5 + 6. The answer key from page 4 is now sealed in an envelope stamped "CHECKED FOR LEAKS," and the wall gauge has gained a shaded error band around its needle. Balloon (Dana): "A range, not a point." Caption: "5 — test for contamination. 6 — show how sure you are."

Panel 5:
Card 7 + 8. Dana and Theo at the whiteboard doing error analysis — sorting wrong answers into labeled piles ("misread", "format", "actually-unsafe") — beside a small drawing linking the test to a real-world use. Caption: "7 — look at the failures by hand. 8 — show the test maps to something that matters."

Panel 6:
Two-shot, quiet. Theo taps the full corkboard with the tape's hook. Balloon (Theo): "None of this makes the number bigger." Dana, faint dry smile: "It makes it true." Caption box: "Slower. Humbler. Honest."
