Page 06

Purpose:
The payoff + irony — an honest benchmark reports a smaller number, with error bars and a defined task, and for the first time it means something.

Audience note:
The landing. Mirror page 1's lobby scene so the change is visible. End on a dry, honest punchline.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
Modern literary graphic-novel art in the vein of understated ligne-claire indie /
alternative comics and New Yorker cover illustration.
Clean ligne-claire linework, thin even ink weight, restrained and precise.
Flat muted color palette: dusty blues, ochre, olive, warm gray; soft print-like tones,
no glossy rendering, no gradients, slight risograph texture.
Naturalistic adult proportions and understated faces; quiet, deadpan, melancholic.
Urban realism — offices, laptops, fluorescent light, late-evening windows.
Dry, ironic, observational comedy. Comedy comes from composition and silence, not
exaggeration.
Not manga chibi. Not cute. Not shonen. Not glossy 3D. Not action lines.
Lettering: clean typeset caption boxes and small naturalistic speech balloons,
New-Yorker-style dry captions.

Characters in this page:
Dana — 34, black bob with blunt bangs, round wire glasses, faint permanent frown, oversized olive cardigan over a plain tee, holding a chipped ceramic mug.
Theo — 38, shaved head, stubble, faded blue work jacket over a gray hoodie.

Setting:
The same corporate lobby as page 1, same fluorescent light, same glossy scale — but now there is a small, plain hand-lettered card taped beneath the readout, and the crowd is gone.

Panel 1:
Wide shot mirroring page 1, but the readout no longer says "94.2". It now reads, in smaller plain type: "SAFE-REFUSAL ON JAILBREAK SET · 61.0 ± 3.4". A taped card beneath: "what this measures · how it was sampled · what it can't tell you." Caption box: "The new number is smaller."

Panel 2:
Close on the readout's error band — a shaded ± range drawn around the figure. Dana looks at it, mug down for once. Balloon (Dana): "It went down twelve points." Theo: "It went up in meaning."

Panel 3:
The lobby is nearly empty — the applauding crowd from page 1 is gone, just two people reading the taped card carefully. Caption box (deadpan): "Honest numbers draw smaller crowds."

Panel 4:
Dana at the podium again, but the slide behind her now shows the defined task and the error bars instead of one giant figure. She looks calm. Balloon (Dana): "I can finally tell you what it does not cover."

Panel 5:
Theo coils his steel tape and rests its hook on the once-sealed crate — now open, its contents clearly labeled. Balloon (Theo): "Same scale. We just made it touch the thing."

Panel 6:
Final quiet wide shot. The two of them leaving, lights low, late evening through the lobby window. The small honest readout glows behind them. Caption box: "It measures less than it used to. For the first time, it measures something."

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions. Keep text areas clear.

Output:
A single finished manga page.
